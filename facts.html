FUN FACTS

Think bricks are just toys? Think again! Here are some fun and surprising facts about Nano Brick sets.

Building complex structures takes time and focus. Bricks teach patience, problem-solving, and planning ahead.
Brick-building is used in autism therapy, rehab, and stress-relief for adults.
Artists and engineers use nano bricks to create interactive art, prototypes, and even robots.
Many adults turn their brick hobby into a career as content creators, freelance builders, or designers.
Bricks don’t go out of date. You can rebuild again and again, across generations.
Some adult fans create custom models, teach classes, or do commissioned work.
People use bricks to act
out scenes, create stories,
and build imaginative worlds.
Nano bricks are much smaller than LEGO. The tiniest pieces are just 4x4x5 mm.
Nano bricks build highly detailed models of animals, instruments, and characters.
The smallest nano brick
piece is almost the size of a
grain of rice.
Nano bricks are often considered more of a display or collector item than a toy.
Nano bricks can help improve fine motor skills and focus due to their tiny size.
The bricks are made from ABS plastic, the same material used in LEGO bricks.
Some builders use tweezers
to help with assembling tiny pieces.
There are musical instrument nano bricks like pianos, violins, and guitars with impressive detail.
Nano bricks are recommended for ages 12 and up, mainly because of the tiny pieces that require patience and precision.
Some nano brick sets come with extra spare pieces, just in case you lose a few during the build.
Some nano brick sets are officially licensed, like Pokémon,
Doraemon, Minions, and
even Sanrio characters.
The tight fit of nano bricks often makes them more stable than LEGO when built correctly.
Nano brick sets are usually cheaper than LEGO and more compact, making them a great choice for collectors with limited shelf space.
